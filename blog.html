Animals Matter To Me (AMTM)

August 10, 2024

AMTM in Mumbai rescued a stray dog named Bruno who had been hit by a vehicle and left on the streets. Severely injured, Bruno was rushed to AMTM’s shelter, where he received immediate medical attention and surgery. Over time, Bruno healed with the care and love provided by the NGO’s dedicated staff. Today, Bruno is a happy, playful dog who found his forever home, thanks to AMTM’s relentless efforts to give him a second chance.

BSPCA recently rescued an injured cow that had been hit by a vehicle on a busy road in Mumbai. The cow was brought to the Bai Sakarbai Dinshaw Petit Hospital for Animals, where she received medical treatment for her wounds and injuries. The hospital's skilled veterinary team nursed her back to health, ensuring her recovery. BSPCA’s efforts showcase their dedication to treating all animals, large and small.

Saahas for Animals

July 27, 2024

In Pune, Saahas rescued a kitten, Meera, who was abandoned on a busy street and left malnourished. The rescue team at Saahas carefully brought her to their facility, where she was nursed back to health with proper nutrition, vaccinations, and round-the-clock care. After her recovery, Meera was adopted into a loving home. Saahas for Animals’ compassionate approach ensured that she got the care and attention needed to grow into a healthy, happy cat.

The Welfare of Stray Dogs (WSD)

August 30, 2024

WSD was alerted to a sick and injured stray dog named Chintu, living on the streets of Mumbai. The team immediately responded, bringing Chintu to their shelter, where he was treated for mange and an infected wound. After weeks of care and recovery, Chintu was not only healed but also vaccinated and neutered, as part of WSD’s commitment to controlling the stray dog population. Chintu is now thriving and has found a forever home through WSD’s adoption drive.

Youth Organization in Defense of Animals (YODA)

November 07, 2024

YODA’s team received a call about a severely dehydrated and injured street dog named Rocky in Mumbai. He was immediately rescued and brought to YODA’s shelter, where he was given medical treatment and care. Rocky’s recovery was slow but steady, and thanks to YODA’s sterilization program, he was neutered and prepared for adoption. Rocky now lives with a caring family, embodying the impact of YODA’s tireless dedication.

Resq Charitable Trust (Pune)

April 25, 2024

One night, Resq’s 24/7 emergency helpline received a distress call about a deer trapped in a residential area. The wildlife rescue team quickly responded, safely relocating the scared and injured deer to their dedicated wildlife rescue facility. The deer was treated for minor injuries, rehabilitated, and eventually released back into the wild—a testament to Resq’s expertise in wildlife rescues.
